Definition

Nation-state hackers are individuals or groups that are sponsored or affiliated with a government and carry out cyber attacks on other countries or organizations. They are typically highly skilled and well-funded, and their attacks are often politically motivated. They are also known as state-sponsored hackers.

Details

A nation-state hacker refers to an individual or group that is sponsored or supported by a government and carries out cyber attacks on other countries or entities. These hackers are typically highly skilled and well-funded, and their primary goal is to gather intelligence, steal sensitive information, disrupt critical infrastructure, or conduct espionage on behalf of their country. Some examples of nation-state hacking groups are APT28 and APT29, also known as "Fancy Bear" and "Cozy Bear" which have been attributed to the Russian government. Other examples are Lazarus group and DarkHotel, which have been linked to North Korea. Nation-state hacking is a serious concern for governments and organizations around the world, as these attacks can have significant consequences for national security, economic stability, and individual privacy.

Motive

  • • Gathering intelligence: Nation-state hackers may target other countries or organizations to gain access to sensitive information such as military plans, diplomatic communications, or economic data.
  • • Stealing sensitive information: Nation-state hackers may target individuals, organizations, or companies to steal sensitive information such as trade secrets, personal information, or financial data.
  • • Disrupting critical infrastructure: Nation-state hackers may target critical infrastructure such as power plants, transportation systems, or financial networks to disrupt operations and cause chaos.
  • • Conducting espionage: Nation-state hackers may target other governments, military, and other organizations to spy on their activities and gain intelligence advantage.
  • • Sabotage: Nation-state hackers may attempt to disrupt and sabotage the operations of other countries or organizations by attacking or destroying important systems or networks.
