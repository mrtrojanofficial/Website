Script-Kiddies

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition

Script kiddies are individuals who use pre-existing scripts or tools to carry out hacking or cyber attacks, often without a deep understanding of how they work and with less technical skills than professional hackers, they might cause harm to networks, websites, or individuals.

Details

Script kiddies are individuals who use pre-existing scripts or tools to conduct cyber attacks, often without a deep understanding of how they work. They are considered less skilled and experienced than professional hackers. They lack the knowledge and the ability to write their own code and scripts, instead they use tools and scripts developed by others. They are often motivated by mischief, attention seeking or the desire to cause disruption. They may not have the same level of sophistication or resources as nation-state hackers or cyber criminals. Script kiddies can cause harm to networks, websites, or individuals, although their attacks are usually less sophisticated and less damaging than those of professional hackers. They can also be considered as a nuisance and a cause of extra work for security professionals. They are often associated with hacking groups and communities that share and promote the use of hacking tools, scripts and tutorials.

Motive

  • • Mischief: They may want to cause disruption or annoyance for the sake of it.
  • • Attention seeking: They may want to be recognized for their hacking activities.
  • • Thrill seeking: They may want to experience the rush of successfully hacking into a system.
  • • Vandalism: They may want to damage or deface a website or network.
  • • Fame: They may want to be known as a skilled hacker in a hacking community or group.
