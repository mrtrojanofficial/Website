Whistle-blowers

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition

A whistleblower is an individual who exposes illegal, unethical, or corrupt practices within an organization, government or agency. They reveal information to the public or to those in positions of authority in order to stop the illegal or unethical activity. They may act out of a sense of moral or civic duty and may be protected by laws against retaliation.

Details

A whistleblower is an individual who exposes illegal, unethical, or corrupt practices within an organization, government or agency. They reveal information to the public or to those in positions of authority in order to stop the illegal or unethical activity. They can be an employee, ex-employee or even a contractor of the organization, and they reveal the information despite the risk that it may pose to their career, relationships or even personal safety. Whistleblowers often have access to sensitive information, and they choose to reveal it in order to hold organizations accountable, to protect public interest, to fight against corruption or to promote transparency. They can act out of a sense of moral or civic duty, and in many cases, they may be protected by laws against retaliation.

Motive

  • • To expose illegal or unethical behavior
  • • To bring attention to issues that are not being addressed
  • • To hold individuals or organizations accountable for their actions
  • • To promote transparency and integrity
  • • To make a positive impact on society and protect the public interest.
