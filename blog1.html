What is Cyber Security ?

Define

Cyber security is a term that you may have heard before, but you may not be exactly sure what it means or why it is important. Simply put, cyber security refers to the measures taken to protect computer systems and networks from cyber attacks, theft, damage, or unauthorised access

Explain

In today's digital age, where most of our daily activities are done online, cyber security has become a crucial aspect of our lives. With the rise of digital technology and the internet, we have become increasingly dependent on computers and mobile devices for communication, work, entertainment, and financial transactions. However, this increased reliance on technology has also made us vulnerable to various cyber threats that can compromise our personal and sensitive information.

Cyber Attacks

Cyber attacks can take many forms and come from different sources, including hackers, cybercriminals, state-sponsored groups, and even insiders. Some common types of cyber attacks include phishing, malware, ransomware, denial-of-service attacks, and social engineering. These attacks can cause serious harm to individuals, businesses, and organisations, including financial loss, identity theft, reputational damage, and legal consequences.

Protection

To protect against these threats, cyber security professionals employ various strategies and technologies to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of computer systems and networks. Here are some of the key components of cyber security that you should be aware of:

● Confidentiality: Confidentiality refers to the protection of sensitive and confidential information from unauthorised access or disclosure. This can be achieved through encryption, access control, and data masking techniques. For example, when you enter your password to log in to your online banking account, the system uses encryption to keep your password and account information secure from potential eavesdropping or interception.

● Integrity: Integrity refers to the accuracy, completeness, and reliability of data and information. Cyber security measures are put in place to ensure that data is not altered, deleted, or tampered with by unauthorised individuals or malware. This can be achieved through techniques such as digital signatures, checksums, and integrity checks.

● Availability: Availability refers to the ability of computer systems and networks to be accessible and operational at all times. Cyber security measures aim toprevent or mitigate disruptions to computer systems and networks caused by cyber attacks or other incidents. This can be achieved through measures such as backup and recovery procedures, redundancy, and disaster recovery plans.

● Education and awareness: Education and awareness refer to the importance of educating and training individuals on cyber security best practices and raising awareness of cyber threats. This includes teaching individuals how to identify and avoid phishing emails, how to create strong passwords, and how to secure their personal devices and networks.

Now that you have a better understanding of what cyber security is and its importance, it's important to take steps to protect yourself and your personal information online. Here are some simple tips to improve your cyber security:

Pro Tip

1. Use a password manager to create and store strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts.

2. Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
