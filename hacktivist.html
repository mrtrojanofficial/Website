Hacktivists

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition

Hacktivists are individuals or groups who use hacking as a form of political activism. They typically target government, corporations, or other organizations with the aim of drawing attention to a specific cause or issue. They often use DDoS, website defacement and data breaches to achieve their goals.

Details

Hacktivism is a form of cybercrime and it is illegal in most countries, and the actions of hacktivists can have serious consequences for the targeted organizations and individuals. Hacktivists can be state-sponsored or non-state actors, and their actions can have serious implications for national security and foreign relations. Hacktivists usually use the Internet as a platform to reach a wider audience and to amplify the effects of their actions, but they can also use other technologies like social media, mobile phones, and even radio and television.

Motive

  • • Political activism: They may want to draw attention to a specific cause or issue.
  • • Protest: They may want to protest against government policies or actions.
  • • Freedom of speech: They may want to promote freedom of speech or the freedom of information.
  • • Holding organizations accountable: They may want to expose illegal or unethical practices within organizations.
  • • Anarchism: They may want to challenge the authority of governments and corporations.
